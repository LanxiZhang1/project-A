Basic Analysis

According the histogram of the ratings of top 200 movies, a roughly normal distribution slightly skewed towards higher ratings. The peak is around rating range between 7 and 8, suggesting the majority of popular movies have average ratings within the interval. However, there are few moviews has very low ratings below 5.

Then we will do the network and textual analysis to further examine the data.

Network Analysis

The network is created by presenting actors as nodes and connections between the actors who have appeared in the same movie as edges. There are total number of 8058 nodes and 301920 edges in the network.

Density: 0.0093
Number of connected components: 23
Number of isolated nodes: 0

The network has relatively low density, suggesting that the network is not totally interconnected. The number of connected components and the isolated nodes indicates that there are many actors from 23 movies have not appeared in other movies and all the movies have more than one actors.

Average of degree: 74.937
Median of degree: 62.0
Minimum of degree: 2
Maximum of degree: 455

Each actor has collaborated with around 75 other actors on average, showing a high level of connectivity among actors. And the significant difference between median and mean value suggests that there has the actors who has better influential than others. The Maximum degree indicates the presence of actors who are involved in many movies, which could reveal the insights of the relatinship between actors and movie ratings.

Thus, compared to other actors, the audiences might be more familiar with them, and tend tto have more influence and a larger fan base at same time.

Overall, there does appear to be some relationship between actors and ratings. Actors who are more selective about their roles are more likely to appear in high rating movies. And actors with high popularity are more offten to attract better production teams, leading to high rated movies. Additionally, their fans might inclined to give higher votes to the films. However, the result suggests a potential trade-off between quantity and quality. Therefore, multiple detailed indicators such as acting skills, should be considered in further research.
